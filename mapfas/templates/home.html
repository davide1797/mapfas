🧪 PFAS

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are synthetic chemical compounds used widely in everyday products. Also named "forever chemicals", they are persistent in the environment, due to the interactions between carbon and fluorine, and pose risks to human health, animals, and plants. PFAS can be found in firefighting foams, non-stick cookware, water-repellent clothing, stain-resistant fabrics, some cosmetics, and grease- or water-resistant products.
